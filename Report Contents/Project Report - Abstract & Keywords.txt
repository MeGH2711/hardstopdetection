This report presents a comprehensive framework for the analysis of hard-stop and momentary-stop, which is critical for enhancing traffic safety and also prevents rear-end collisions. The system extracts precise telemetry data, including object classification, spatial coordinates, and instantaneous velocity, by utilizing a high-altitude perspective. By preprocessing the spatial coordinates and altitude-compensated displacement of the vehicle, the system calculates precise velocity and deceleration/acceleration profiles. We define a "hard stop" as a rapid negative acceleration exceeding a critical safety threshold, while "momentary stops" are identified through zero-velocity duration analysis. The experimental results, derived from a dataset of vehicle trajectories captured at a stabilized flight height, demonstrate that the proposed method can effectively categorize stop-and-go patterns and hazardous braking events. This framework provides a scalable solution for Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS) to monitor road safety and traffic flow dynamics in real-time.
